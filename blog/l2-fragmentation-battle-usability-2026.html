L2 Fragmentation 2026: 100+ Rollups, Tapi Siapa yang Benar-Benar Dipakai?

2026-06-22 · 8 min read
layer 2 rollup ethereum scaling web3

Ethereum's rollup-centric roadmap udah deliver janji scalability-nya — 100+ L2 networks sekarang live, dengan total TVL sekitar $40.94 billion. Tapi ada masalah besar yang ga banyak dibahas: fragmentation. Liquidity pecah, user experience confusing, dan security surface area makin lebar. Welcome to the L2 fragmentation era.

Di post ini, gue breakdown L2 landscape 2026 — siapa yang beneran dipake, kenapa fragmentation jadi masalah critical, dan apa artinya buat security researchers dan DeFi users.

Angkanya impressive. Tapi kalau lo coba pake 5 L2 berbeda dalam satu hari, lo bakal ngerti kenapa user experience-nya ga optimal. Bridging takes time, gas token beda-beda, dan wallet behavior inconsistent across chains.

The Big Five: Siapa yang Dominan?

1. Arbitrum One

Masih jadi king of L2 dengan TVL terbesar. Optimistic rollup yang paling mature, ecosystem paling lengkap, dan developer tools paling battle-tested. Kalau lo cuma mau pake satu L2, ini default choice. Tapi sequencer masih centralized — single point of failure yang jadi concern.

2. Optimism (OP Mainnet)

OP Stack jadi standar buat "superchain" vision — banyak chain yang dibangun di atas OP Stack (Base, Zora, Mode). Collective governance model menarik, tapi execution masih trail behind Arbitrum. RetroPGF funding model unik — reward public goods secara retroactive.

3. Base (by Coinbase)

The dark horse. Coinbase backing bikin Base jadi L2 dengan onboarding termudah buat normies. TVL naik gila-gilaan, consumer apps (Farcaster, friend.tech successor) mostly di sini. Trade-off: lebih terpusat, lebih "corporate." Tapi buat adoption? Hard to beat.

4. zkSync Era

ZK-rollup yang ambitious. Native account abstraction, LLVM compiler support. Technically superior tapi developer adoption masih ketinggalan. "Hyperchains" vision (custom ZK chains) menarik tapi belum proven at scale.

5. StarkNet

General-purpose ZK-rollup dengan STARK proofs — theoretically paling secure dan scalable. Cairo language powerful tapi learning curve curam. Ecosystem berkembang tapi masih niche. Buat security researchers, ini area yang paling interesting buat deep dive.

Kenapa Fragmentation Jadi Masalah?

Bayangkan lo punya uang di 5 bank berbeda, dan setiap bank pake mata uang berbeda, ga bisa transfer langsung antar bank, dan fee buat pindahin duit beda-beda tiap saat. That's basically L2 fragmentation sekarang.

Liquidity Fragmentation

$10B liquidity di Arbitrum, $5B di Base, $3B di Optimism — tapi ga bisa diakses seamlessly. DEX liquidity pools terpisah per chain. Slippage lebih tinggi karena pool lebih kecil di setiap chain. Uniswap di Arbitrum punya liquidity berbeda dari Uniswap di Base.

Bridge Risk

Mau pindahin aset antar L2? Harus lewat bridge. Dan bridges udah jadi target #1 buat hackers — billions udah lost dari bridge exploits (Ronin $625M, Wormhole $320M, Nomad $190M). Setiap kali lo bridge, lo nambah risk exposure.

⚠️ Security reality: Setiap L2 punya trust assumptions berbeda. Optimistic rollups rely on fraud proofs (7-day challenge period). ZK rollups rely on validity proofs (lebih aman, tapi complexity lebih tinggi). Lo harus understand model-nya sebelum taruh significant funds.

Wallet UX Chaos

Switch chain di MetaMask, beda gas token (ETH di most L2s, tapi MATIC di Polygon, etc.), different block explorers, inconsistent transaction finality. Buat crypto-native aja confusing — apalagi buat mainstream users.

Security Implications buat Researchers

Fragmentation bukan cuma masalah UX — ini security multiplier. Setiap L2 nambah attack surface:

  • Sequencer centralization — Mayoritas L2 pake single sequencer. Kalau sequencer down, chain stuck. Malicious sequencer bisa censor transactions, extract MEV, atau bahkan reorg blocks.
  • Bridge vulnerabilities — Cross-chain messaging protocols (LayerZero, Wormhole, Axelar) jadi critical infrastructure. Bug di bridge = funds at risk di multiple chains sekaligus.
  • Upgrade risks — Banyak L2 yang masih bisa di-upgrade oleh core team. Proxy contracts bisa di-ganti implementation-nya. Ini necessary buat iteration, tapi juga attack vector.
  • Data availability — Kalau DA layer (Celestia, EigenDA) down atau compromise, L2 transactions ga bisa di-verify. Validiums dan optimiums especially vulnerable.
  • Prover bugs — ZK rollups rely on complex cryptographic proofs. Bug di prover = potential fund loss. StarkNet dan zkSync punya trusted setup atau complex circuits yang perlu deep audit.

Interop Solutions yang Muncul

Industry sadar soal fragmentation problem. Beberapa approaches yang lagi develop:

Shared Sequencing

Projects kayak Espresso, Astria, dan Radius bikin shared sequencer layer yang bisa process transactions untuk multiple L2s sekaligus. Ini enable atomic cross-chain transactions — swap di Arbitrum dan Base dalam satu tx. Still early, tapi promising.

Intent-Based Architectures

Lo declare "I want to swap X for Y" dan solver network figure out the best route across all chains. UniswapX, Across Protocol, dan ANGLE aggregator udah implement ini. User ga perlu tau asetnya di chain mana — intent handles it.

Chain Abstraction

Projects kayak Particle Network dan Socket bikin layer yang abstract away chain differences. Satu wallet, satu balance, semua chains. Ambitious, tapi execution is hard. Kalau berhasil, ini game-changer buat mainstream adoption.

Buat security researchers: Interop protocols = new attack surface yang belum fully explored. Cross-chain message verification, intent solver manipulation, sequencer collusion — ini area yang bounty-nya bakal naik significant dalam 12 bulan ke depan. Start researching sekarang.

Strategi buat Users di Era Fragmentation

  1. Consolidate ke 2-3 L2 max — Jangan spread thin. Pick Arbitrum + Base + satu ZK rollup (zkSync atau StarkNet) buat diversifikasi teknologi.
  2. Gunakan aggregator — Bridge aggregators (Jumper, Bungee, Socket) cariin route terbaik. DEX aggregators (1inch, Paraswap) aggregate liquidity cross-chain.
  3. Audit bridge contracts — Sebelum bridge significant amounts, check apakah bridge-nya udah di-audit, berapa TVL-nya, dan apakah ada incident history.
  4. Monitor sequencer health — Follow L2 status pages. Kalau sequencer sering down, consider migrating funds ke L2 yang lebih reliable.
  5. Account abstraction wallets — Pakai smart wallet (Safe, Biconomy) yang support multi-chain. Session keys dan gas abstraction bikin UX jauh lebih baik.

Prediction: Consolidation is Coming

100+ L2s ga sustainable. Yang bakal terjadi:

  • Winner-take-most — 5-7 L2s bakal dominate. Sisanya either merge, pivot ke niche, atau mati.
  • App-specific chains — Big apps (Uniswap, Aave) mungkin launch their own L2 yang optimized buat use case mereka.
  • L3 emergence — Application-specific layers di atas L2. Further fragmentation? Maybe. Tapi juga better UX karena specialized.
  • Regulation pressure — Compliance requirements bakal force beberapa L2 ke "permissioned" model. Institutional L2s yang KYC-compliant.

Kesimpulan

L2 scaling berhasil secara teknis — Ethereum bisa process jauh lebih banyak transactions sekarang. Tapi scaling tanpa coherent UX = partial success. Fragmentation jadi next big problem yang harus di-solve.

Buat users: consolidate, use aggregators, understand trust assumptions. Buat developers: build interop-first. Buat security researchers: cross-chain attack vectors are the new frontier — bounty pools belum saturated, dan demand bakal skyrocket.

The battle of 2026 bukan lagi L1 vs L2. It's fragmentation vs usability. Dan pemenangnya adalah yang bisa bikin 100+ chains feels like satu chain.
