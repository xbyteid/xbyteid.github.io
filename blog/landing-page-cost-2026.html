How Much Does a Landing Page Cost in 2026?

Everyone wants a landing page. Nobody wants to overpay. Here's what the market actually looks like right now — with real numbers, not vague ranges.

TL;DR

  • DIY (Framer, Webflow, Carrd): $0–$50/mo + your time
  • Budget freelancer (Fiverr, Upwork): $150–$800
  • Professional freelancer: $500–$2,500
  • Small agency: $2,000–$8,000
  • Premium agency: $8,000–$25,000+

The Honest Breakdown

A "landing page" means different things to different people. A single-screen Carrd page and a custom-built, animated, A/B-tested marketing page are both "landing pages" — but they're completely different products.

Here's what you actually get at each price tier:

Tier Price What You Get Timeline
DIY $0–$50/mo Template-based, drag-and-drop. You write copy, choose images, set up forms. Works fine for MVPs. 1–3 days
Budget $150–$800 Modified template or basic custom. Limited revisions. May not be responsive. SEO basics if you're lucky. 3–7 days
Pro Freelancer $500–$2,500 Custom design, responsive, SEO-optimized, contact form, analytics, fast load times. Real code, not a theme. 3–10 days
Small Agency $2,000–$8,000 Custom design + copywriting + SEO strategy. Multiple revisions. Dedicated project manager. May include brand work. 2–4 weeks
Premium Agency $8,000–$25,000+ Full creative direction, custom illustrations, animation, A/B testing setup, conversion optimization, ongoing support. 4–8 weeks

What Actually Affects Price

1. Custom vs. Template

A Fiverr gig for $200 is almost certainly a modified template. That's fine if you just need something online fast. But if you want a design that's actually yours — custom layout, your brand's personality, no cookie-cutter feel — that takes real design and development time.

2. Copywriting

Design is half the battle. The words on your page determine whether visitors convert or bounce. Good copywriting is a separate skill from web development. Some freelancers include basic copy; agencies charge extra for it ($500–$2,000).

3. Responsive + Performance

A proper landing page loads in under 2 seconds, looks perfect on mobile, and passes Core Web Vitals. This isn't hard to do with modern tools — but it requires intentional development, not just "it looks fine on my laptop."

4. SEO Setup

Meta tags, structured data (JSON-LD), Open Graph, canonical URLs, sitemap inclusion. This is table stakes for a professional page, but many budget options skip it entirely.

5. Integrations

Contact forms (Formspree, custom backend), analytics (GA4, Umami), chat widgets, email capture, CRM connections — each integration adds time and complexity.

The Freelancer Sweet Spot

For most startups and small businesses, a professional freelancer ($500–$2,500) hits the best value point. You get:

  • Custom code (not a WordPress theme)
  • Direct communication (no project manager telephone game)
  • Faster turnaround (no agency overhead)
  • Lower cost (no office, no account managers, no sales team)

The biggest myth in web development is that agencies are "safer." A bad agency and a bad freelancer both deliver bad work. A good freelancer with a track record is often the better bet — especially for a single page.

How to Choose

Use DIY tools if you're validating an idea and need something up today. Carrd ($19/yr) or Framer's free tier work great for this.

Hire a budget freelancer if you have a tight budget and a clear vision. Provide detailed requirements, reference designs, and all copy upfront.

Hire a pro freelancer if you want something that looks professional and converts. This is the sweet spot for 80% of landing page needs.

Go with an agency if you need strategy, copywriting, design, and development under one roof — and you have the budget for it.
